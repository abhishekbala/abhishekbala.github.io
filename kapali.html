Kapaleeswarar Brahmotsavam Vahanam

Shiva is worshipped at Kapaleeswarar at the Kapaleeswarar Temple in Mylapore, Chennai. Shiva's consort Parvathi is worshipped as Karpagambal.

This location is significant for many reasons. Goddess Karpagambal did penance here to restore her personality after a curse. Murugan received the Sakthi Vel spear to destroy a demon here. Brahma worshipped here to get rid of his ego. Lesser known stories include that Rama came here to pray before his war against Ravana.

The name Kapaleeswarar comes from Kapalam (head) and Eeswarar (an alias of Lord Shiva). When Brahma failed to show respect to Shiva, Shiva plucked one of his heads (kapalams). In an act of penance, Brahma descended upon Mylapore and installed a lingam to please Shiva.

In the 20th century, the famous Carnatic musician Papanasam Sivan would walk around the Kapaleeswarar temple and compose gems on Kapaleeswarar and Karpagambal. One of his masterpieces is Kapali in Raga Mohanam and Adi Talam.
