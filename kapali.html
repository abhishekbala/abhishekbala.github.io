Surutapalli Neelakantam Bhajeham

Description

Legend has it that during the churning of the oceans by the devas and asuras, in addition to the holy nectar, the poison Halahala rose from the ocean. To save the world from the poison, Shiva consumed it. When the poison was going down his throat, Parvati stopped it there by tightly holding it, and thus his throat turned blue.

On his return journey to his abode at Kailash, Shiva felt exhausted and took rest at Surutapalli in Andhra. He lay with his lap on Parvati. This lying posture of Shiva is rare and can be seen in the Pallikondeswara Temple of Surutapalli.

The song Neelakantam Bhajeham, composed by Muthuswami Dikshitar, describes Lord Shiva as the nIlakaNTham, or the blue-throated one.
