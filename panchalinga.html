Panchabhootha Sthalam

Ekambareshwarar Temple Land

The Ekambareshwarar Temple in Kanchipuram features the Prithivi Lingam. This temple is associated with the Earth element.

When Parvati was cursed to become dark like Kali, she did penance under the temple's ancient Mango tree. Parvati made a Shiva Linga out of sand to unite with Shiva.

Muthuswami Dikshitar's Chintaya Ma Kanda in Raga Bhairavi and Rupaka Talam was composed at this temple.

Thirunaikaval Temple Water

The Jambukeshwarar Temple in Thiruvanaikaval (near Trichy) features the Appu (Jambu) Lingam. The sanctum has an underground water stream and in spite of pumping water out, it is always filled with water.

Muthuswami Dikshitar composed Jamupathe set to Yamuna Kalyani in Tisra Jathi Eka Talam.

Arunachaleshwara Temple Temple Fire

The Annamalaiyar Temple in Thiruvannamalai is located at the base of the Annamalai hills. The hill behind the temple is sacred and considered a lingam (manifestation of Lord Shiva) itself. This temple is one of the largest in India.

Shiva is worshipped here as Arunachaleshwarar as the agni lingam, agni meaning fire.

Muthuswami Dikshitar composed Arunachala Natham set to Saranga Ragam and Tisra Jathi Eka Talam here.

Sri Kalahasteeshwara Temple Air

The Srikalahasti Temple is located in the town of Srikalahasti in Andhra Pradesh. Shiva is worshipped here as the Vayu lingam, vayu meaning wind.

The famous story here is that of Kannappa Nayanar, a hunter who was a staunch of the Vayu Linga of Srikalahasti. Once, Shiva tested his devotion by causing blood to ooze out of the linga's eyes. Kannappa desperately plucked out his own eye and placed in the spot of the bleeding eye and covered the other eye with his foot to stop the bleeding. Shiva, moved by his devotion, appeared before him and restored his eyes.

Muthuswami Dikshitar composed Sri Kalahastisha set to Huseni Ragam and Kanda Chapu Talam.

Thillai Natarajar Temple Space

The Thillai Natarajar Temple is located in Chidambaram. This temple is known as the foremost of all temples in Shaivism. Shiva is worshipped here in two forms - one as a crystallized lingam, and the other as empty space. The deity here is Thillai Natajara - Shiva, the Lord of Dance. Shiva's consort here is Sivakami Amman.

Muthuswami Dikshitar composed Ananda Natana Prakasham set to Kedaram Ragam and Misra Chapu Talam here.
