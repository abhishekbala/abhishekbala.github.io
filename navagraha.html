Surya Suryamurthe Namostute

Surya or Ravi is the first of the nine heavenly houses (Navagraha) in Hindu astrology. It is the basis of Ravivara, or Sunday, in the Hindu calendar. Major festivals and pilgrimages in reverence of Surya include Makar Sankranti, Pongal, Ratha Sapthami, and Kumbh Mela.

Suryamurthe Namostute in Raga Saurashtram in Chatusra Jathi Dhruva Talam is the first of Muthuswami Dikshitar's Navagraha krithis.

Mangala Angarakam Ashrayamyaham

Mangala is the name for Mars. He is the God of War and Celibacy and is often linked to Muruga. Mangala is the basis of Mangalavara or Tuesday in the Hindu calendar.

Angarakam Ashrayamyaham in Raga Suruti in Rupaka Talam is the third of Muthuswami Dikshitar's Navagraha krithis.

Brihaspati Brhaspate Tarapate

Brihaspati refers to Jupiter, and is the root of the word "Brihaspativara" or Thursday in the Hindu calendar. Brihaspati is known as the Teacher of the Gods.

Shukra Shri Shukra Bhagavantam

Shukra refers to Venus, and is the root of the word "Shukravara" or Friday in the Hindu calendar. Shukracharya was the guru of the Asuras and Daityas. In the Mahabharatha, Shukracharya was mentioned as one of the mentors of Bhishma. Furthermore, Shukra is said to have divided himself into two, each half providing knowledge to the Devas (gods) and Asuras (demons).

Sri Shukra Bhagavantam in Raga Paras in Khanda Jathi Ata Talam is the sixth of Muthuswami Dikshitar's Navagraha krithis.

Rahu Smaramyaham Sada Rahum

Rahu is the severed head of an asura called Svarbhanu that swallows the sun causing eclipses. Rahu is one of the nine planets in Vedic astrology and is paired with Ketu. The time of day associated with Rahu is said to be inauspicious.

Smaramyaham in Raga Ramapriya in Rupakam Talam is the eight of Muthuswami Dikshitar's Navagraha krithis.
