Muruga & Ganesha doing Shiva Pooja

Muruga

Muruga, also known as Karthikeya, Skanda, Kumara, and Subramanya, is the Hindu God of War. He is the son of Shiva and Parvathi and the brother of Ganesha. Muruga is typically represented as a young man riding a peacock and carrying a spear.

The song, Sri Valli Devasenapathe, is a composition of Papanasam Sivan set to Natabhairavi Ragam and Adi Talam.

Pallavi
shrI vallI dEva sEnApatE shrI subramaNyA namOstutE
Anupallavi
dEvathA sArva bhauma jaya jaya tavi shaD-bhuja kArtikEyA mEyA
Charanam
mAmava sadA shivakumArA vimAnI krta citra mayUrA shrta
kAmita phala dAyaka haThashUra karuNA jaladhara jagadAdhAra



Ganesha

Ganesha, also known as Vinayakar or Ganapati, is one of the best-known and most worshipped deities in Hinduism. Ganesha is the Elephant God and Remover of Obstacles.

The famous song, Maha Ganapatim, is a composition of Muthuswami Dikshitar set to Nattai Ragam and Eka Talam.

Pallavi
mahA gaNa patiM manasA smarAmi
vasishTha vAma dEva-Adi vandita
samashTi caraNam
mahA dEva sutaM guru guha nutaM
mAra kOTi prakASaM SAntaM
mahA kAvya nATaka-Adi priyaM
mUshika vAhana mOdaka priyam
